# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Busy classroom teachers. They capture student data — assessment sheets, IEP goal
progress, behaviour logs, sticky-note observations — often handwritten, often
mid-day between lessons. The capture moment is fast and mobile (snap a photo);
the review moment is calmer and usually on a desktop. They are not data analysts;
they want their paper records turned into structured data without having to
re-type anything, and they need to trust what comes out.

## Product Purpose

A teacher data co-pilot that turns photos of paper teaching records into
structured, reviewable data. AI extracts the content; the teacher reviews,
corrects, and approves before anything is finalised (human-in-the-loop is a hard
requirement, not a nicety). Success is when a teacher uploads a sheet, sees most
of it already filled in correctly, fixes at most one field, and approves — far
faster than starting from a blank form.

## Brand Personality

Efficient and sharp. The tool is fast, precise, and gets out of the way. Three
words: **capable, quiet, exact.** It should feel like a well-made instrument a
professional reaches for — confident without being loud, helpful without being
chatty. The voice is plain and direct; no exclamation marks, no cheerleading.

## Anti-references

- **Childish edu-apps.** No cartoon mascots, no bright primary-color blocks, no
  gamified badges/streaks/confetti. This is a professional tool for adults doing
  serious work.
- **AI-hype aesthetics.** No purple gradients, no glassmorphism, no sparkle/"magic"
  iconography, no "powered by AI" theatrics. The AI is infrastructure, not a
  selling point shoved in the user's face.
- **Clinical enterprise SaaS** dense grids and cold navy-gray admin panels are also
  off-brand, though the bigger risks are the two above.

## Design Principles

1. **Extraction is a draft, not an answer.** Every AI output is presented as
   something the teacher confirms or corrects. Never imply finality the human
   hasn't granted.
2. **Surface uncertainty, don't hide it.** Low-confidence fields and ambiguities
   become actionable inputs (a flag the teacher resolves in one tap), not silent
   guesses or buried warnings.
3. **The fast path is the photo.** Capture must be near-frictionless; review can
   afford more density and care because it happens on a calmer surface.
4. **Partial wins count.** Getting most fields right so the teacher fills one
   blank beats demanding a perfect parse. Design for graceful partial results.
5. **Quiet by default.** Restraint over decoration. The interface earns trust by
   being legible and calm, not by showing off.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Best-effort, leaning on sensible defaults: legible body contrast (≥4.5:1),
keyboard-operable controls, and a `prefers-reduced-motion` alternative for any
motion. No formal WCAG commitment, but no careless contrast or motion traps
either — teachers use this all day, often on cheap school hardware.
